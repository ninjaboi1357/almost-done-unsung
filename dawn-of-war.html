Dawn of War

In 1937, Japanese expansionism met militarism and sparked the start of the Second Sino-Japanese War, turning American opinion against Japan. Additionally, in late 1937, Japanese aircraft attacked the USS Panay, killing three Americans. While this event didn’t start a war, it furthered tensions.



In 1939, the Danzig Crisis in Poland brought about the start of the Second World War in Europe. Emboldened by the signing of the Tripartite Pact in 1940, a defensive military alliance between Germany, Italy, and Japan, Japan furthered its aggression in the Pacific. In retaliation, America froze all Japanese assets and embargoed oil exports to Japan.



Aware of its limited resources, the Japanese Empire decided that a decisive attack was required to destroy the American Navy before those resources ran out. Following the failure of the last negotiations in 1941, a surprise attack on Pearl Harbor was launched by Japan, bringing the “sleeping giant” into an expanding war.
